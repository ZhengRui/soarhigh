# Toastmasters Roles

This skill explains the standard Toastmasters meeting roles and the education path. SoarHigh follows the Toastmasters International role conventions; club-specific assignment policy lives in `soarhigh-bylaws`.

> **For sample scripts (参考话术) — what each role-taker actually SAYS during the meeting (opening lines, transitions, reports) — load `toastmasters-role-scripts`.** That companion skill covers SAA / President / Timer / Grammarian / AhCounter / Hark Master / Guest Intro Host / TTM / Moment of Truth scripts. This skill stays focused on what each role IS and DOES.

## Functional roles (every meeting)

### Toastmaster of the Meeting (TOM, TM)
The MC of the meeting. Introduces the agenda, hands off between segments, and keeps the meeting flowing. The TOM does NOT evaluate speeches; they orchestrate. Often confused with **Meeting Manager** — different role (see below).

### Table Topics Master (TTM)
Runs the impromptu speaking segment. Picks a theme, prepares ~9 prompts / questions, and invites everyone present who is **not** the TTE — guests AND members alike — to take the floor and respond. The TTM also delivers a brief Table Topics Opening (which the TTE evaluates as part of the TT block).

> **SoarHigh note:** at SoarHigh the TTM is typically the **Meeting Manager** for that meeting (same person plans the agenda + runs TT). See `soarhigh-bylaws` for club-specific role conventions.

### Table Topics Speaker (TTS, TT)
Anyone in the room who isn't the TTE — guest or member — called or volunteered by the TTM to deliver an impromptu speech responding to the prompt. Bell band is 1:00 / 1:30 / 2:00; speeches are **typically delivered at the full 2 minutes**, but when the segment has a lot of speakers or is running tight on time, the TTM may cap speakers at the 1:30 yellow. **TT** is most often used as shorthand for the *segment* itself ("Table Topics") in chat and agendas.

### Prepared Speaker
A member delivering a prepared speech from their Pathways project. Speech length depends on the project (typically 5–7 minutes for early projects, up to 20+ for advanced).

### Individual Evaluator (IE)
Evaluates one specific Prepared Speaker — anchored to that speech's Pathways project objectives, ~3 minutes per speaker.

### Table Topics Evaluator (TTE)
Evaluates the Table Topics segment **speaker by speaker** (TTM's Opening + each TT speaker individually) — total time 5–8 minutes for the whole block.

### General Evaluator (GE)
Evaluates the meeting itself, focused on three role-groups (Facilitators / Hosts / Evaluators) plus overall agenda flow + atmosphere — total time 5–8 minutes. Often the most experienced evaluator in the room.

> **The full evaluation methodology — sandwich structure, address-the-audience-first convention, Pathways project-objective anchoring, per-role prep, common pitfalls, evaluation contests — lives in `toastmasters-evaluation`.** Load that skill for "how do I evaluate well?" / "我做 IE 怎么准备?" type questions. This section only identifies the three roles.

### Grammarian
Tracks language use. Generically: picks the "word of the day" before the meeting and challenges members to use it. Listens for grammatical errors, awkward phrasing, and standout language ("rhetorical gem of the day"). Reports observations near the end. Typically 2–3 minutes.

> **SoarHigh note:** at SoarHigh the **Word of the Day is picked by the Meeting Manager** (typically also the TTM) and **announced by the TTM** as part of their TT Opening. The TOM and Grammarian may also reinforce it during transitions or in the Grammarian's report — but the formal announcement is the TTM's. The Grammarian still tracks usage during the meeting and reports counts at the end. See `soarhigh-bylaws` for club-specific role conventions.

### AhCounter
Counts filler words ("ah", "um", "you know", "like", "actually"). Reports counts per speaker near the end of the meeting. Typically **1–2 minutes** at SoarHigh (bell band 1:00 / 1:30 / 2:00 — see `meeting-protocol` for the canonical rule). (Some clubs combine Grammarian + AhCounter into one role.)

### Timer
Operates the timing lights / bells: **green** at minimum time, **yellow** at target, **red** at maximum. After red, the speaker has a **30-second grace period to conclude**; once that grace expires the Timer **rings a bell** and the speaker must leave the stage. Reports each speaker's actual duration near the end of the meeting.

**Enforcement of the bell differs by setting:**
- **Formal Toastmasters contests:** the Timer does NOT ring the bell (silent timing), but any speech that runs past the red+30s bell threshold is **disqualified** from the contest.
- **Regular club meetings (including SoarHigh):** the bell is rung as above, but it's a **soft requirement** — speakers who run over still participate in the meeting's awards voting. The bell mainly signals time discipline, not disqualification.

### Hark Master (Listener / Hark)
Asks recall questions about content from the meeting at the end ("Who mentioned a story about hiking? What was their main point?"). Tests that the audience was attentive. Optional in some clubs; common in clubs using a richer agenda.

> **SoarHigh note:** at SoarHigh the Hark Master rewards **the first audience member to answer each question correctly with snacks** — a small in-meeting prize that keeps the audience engaged through the recall segment. See `soarhigh-bylaws` for club-specific role conventions.

### Sergeant At Arms (SAA)
The first voice of the meeting. Introduces the meeting rules at the start (no phones, mute when not speaking, mobile-on-silent, etc.) and **officially declares the meeting open**, then hands off to the President / TOM for the rest of the agenda. Also typically handles physical / virtual setup logistics before the meeting begins.

### President / Opening Remarks
The club president (or designated officer) opens with brief remarks. The **primary purpose** is to introduce **Toastmasters International** (what TM is, what members do) and **the local club itself** (its identity, culture, what newcomers can expect) — especially for the benefit of any guests in the room. May also touch on the meeting theme, announcements, and recognitions. 1–2 minutes.

### Guests Self Introduction Host
Opens by **defining who counts as a "guest"** for the audience: **anyone present who is NOT a paid club member is a guest** (visitors, prospective members, returning alumni who let membership lapse, friends of members — all guests). Then invites each guest to introduce themselves briefly (typically 30–60 seconds: name, where they're from, how they heard about the club, why they came). The whole segment is **typically 5–8 minutes**, depending on how many guests are present. Only relevant when guests are present.

### Moment of Truth (MoT)
Generic Toastmasters convention: a quality-assurance segment near the end that collects feedback from members on the meeting itself ("what was great", "what could be better") so officers can improve future meetings. Not always run.

> **SoarHigh note:** at SoarHigh the MoT segment is repurposed as **真情分享 ("heart-to-heart sharing")** — an open invitation for **anyone present** to come on stage and share any feelings, reflections, or stories they want, on any topic (not limited to meeting feedback). It functions as an emotional / human-connection close to the meeting rather than as a QA feedback channel. The label in agendas is still `Moment of Truth` / `MoT`. See `soarhigh-bylaws` for club-specific role conventions.

## Workshop-style meetings

Replace some of the above with:

### Workshop Speaker
A guest or member delivering an extended educational session (skills training, deep-dive on a Pathways concept, etc.) — typically 30–60 minutes. Workshop meetings drop the prepared-speech / TT / IE structure and center on the workshop content.

## Officer roles (year-round)

These are NOT meeting-segment roles; they're the club's leadership team:

- **President** — overall leadership, sets vision.
- **VP Education (VPE)** — owns members' Pathways progression, schedules speeches, mentors.
- **VP Membership (VPM)** — recruitment, guest follow-up, member retention.
- **VP Public Relations (VPPR)** — promotion, social media, brand.
- **Secretary** — meeting minutes, records.
- **Treasurer** — finances, dues.
- **Sergeant At Arms** — physical/virtual setup, logistics.

Officer roles run on a 1-year term in most clubs (July 1 – June 30 program year). Some clubs run 6-month terms instead.

> **SoarHigh note:** SoarHigh runs **6-month terms** (Jul–Dec and Jan–Jun), with elections held twice a year. See `soarhigh-bylaws` for the current officer roster and term details.

## Meeting Manager — IMPORTANT distinction

**Meeting Manager** (会议经理) is NOT a meeting-segment role. The Meeting Manager is the person who plans and organizes a specific meeting before it happens — recruits the TOM, TTM, evaluators, etc., picks the theme, drafts the agenda. On meeting day they may also serve as TOM, TTM, or just attend. The "manager" role is logistical/organizational, performed mostly in advance.

When users say "X 主持" (X hosted), they typically mean an in-meeting hosting role (TOM, TTM, MoT, etc.), NOT Meeting Manager. When users say "X 组织 / 担任会议经理" (X organized / served as Meeting Manager), that's the Meeting Manager role.

> **SoarHigh note:** for the practical week-by-week MM workflow at SoarHigh (theme drafting, recruitment posts, role-guide push, agenda + PPT prep, day-of refreshments, who to contact for what), see `soarhigh-meeting-manager`. This section only covers the role *definition*.

## The Pathways education program (brief)

Pathways is Toastmasters International's modern education curriculum (introduced in 2018, replacing the legacy CC/AC tracks). Members pick a "path" focused on a skill area and complete projects within five progressive levels (Mastering Fundamentals → Demonstrating Expertise). Each project is a prepared speech with stated learning objectives, and the IE evaluates against THOSE objectives — not generic speech criteria.

The number of active specialized paths and their names change over time as Toastmasters International updates the curriculum. **For the current path list, level structure, project mechanics, mentor system, and how Pathways speeches show up in agendas (e.g. `Joyce Feng (DL L1P2)`), load `toastmasters-pathways`.** This section deliberately does not duplicate that content here, so the path list stays consistent with one source.


## Awards (typical SoarHigh categories — see soarhigh-bylaws for current list)

- **Best Prepared Speaker (BestPS)** — best Prepared Speech of the meeting.
- **Best Table Topic Speaker (BestTTS)** — best impromptu.
- **Best Evaluator (BestEvaluator)** — best evaluation across IE/TTE/GE.
- **Best Host (BestHost)** — best in-meeting hosting role (TOM, TTM, etc.).
- **Best Facilitator (BestFacilitator)** — best in SAA / Timer / Grammarian / AhCounter / Hark.
- **Best Supporter (BestSupporter)** — most engaged member outside formal roles.
- **Best Meeting Manager (BestMM)** — best organization of the meeting itself.

Award winners are voted by the audience near the end of the meeting.
