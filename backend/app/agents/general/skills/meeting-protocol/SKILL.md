# Toastmasters Meeting Protocol

Generic Toastmasters meeting structure and conventions. Most clubs follow this flow with minor variations; club-specific timings live in `soarhigh-bylaws`.

## Standard Regular meeting flow

A typical Regular meeting runs **~2 hours** at SoarHigh (~15 min pre-meeting warm-up + ~105 min from official start to close). Standard segment order:

1. **Pre-meeting registration & warm-up** (~15 min before official start) — members and guests arrive, sign in, get name tags, mingle informally.
2. **SAA opens** — introduces meeting rules (phones on silent, mute when not speaking, etc.) and **officially declares the meeting open** (1–2 min).
3. **President's opening remarks** — primarily introduces Toastmasters International + the local club (for guests' benefit); may also cover theme + announcements (1–2 min).
4. **TOM (Toastmaster of the Meeting) introduction** — sets the meeting tone, introduces the theme in depth, frames the agenda (2–3 min).
5. **Timer introduction** — Timer explains the bell rules (Table Topics 1:00/1:30/2:00, Prepared Speech 5:00/6:00/7:00, grace + bell at red+30s) (2 min).
6. **Grammarian introduction** — Grammarian introduces themselves and explains their role (track language usage, standout phrases, grammar errors). Mentions tonight's Word of the Day. (1–2 min)
7. **AhCounter introduction** — AhCounter introduces themselves and explains their role (track filler words). (1 min)
8. **Hark Master introduction** — Hark Master sets up the listening segment + previews the snack reward (1 min).
9. **Guest self-introductions** — Guest Intro Host first defines what a "guest" is (anyone NOT a paid club member), then invites each guest to introduce themselves briefly (~30–60 sec each). Whole segment 5–8 min depending on guest count; only if guests present.
10. **TTM Opening + Table Topic Session** — TTM introduces themselves, **announces the Word of the Day** (reinforcing what the Grammarian said earlier), frames the TT theme, then runs ~9 impromptu prompts. Each TT speaker has 2 min (capped at 1:30 yellow if many speakers / tight time). The TTM invites everyone present who isn't the TTE — guests + members alike — to take the floor. **Whole segment 18–20 min total** (TTM opening + ~9 speakers).
11. **Prepared Speeches** — typically **2 or 3 speeches** at SoarHigh. TOM introduces each speaker (path / project / title); speaker delivers; 5–7 min for early Pathways projects, longer for advanced. Each PS is followed later by its own IE (step 14 below) — so 2 PS → 2 IEs, 3 PS → 3 IEs.
12. **Tea break & group photos** — 10 min refreshments + group photo with guests.
13. **Table Topics Evaluation (TTE)** — 5–8 min total, evaluating each TT speaker (and the TTM's Opening) individually, not a single whole-segment summary.
14. **Individual Evaluations (IE)** — one per Prepared Speaker, ~3 min each (bell band 2:00/2:30/3:00; in practice IEs almost always run to the 3-min red).
15. **Timer's Report** — actual durations per speaker, who hit / missed bell windows (2–3 min).
16. **Grammarian's Report** — Word of the Day usage counts + standout language + grammar observations (2–3 min).
17. **AhCounter's Report** — filler-word counts per speaker (1–2 min).
18. **Hark Master Pop Quiz Time** — recall questions for the audience; **at SoarHigh, first correct answer wins a snack** (3–5 min).
19. **General Evaluation (GE)** — 5–8 min total. Evaluates three role-groups (Facilitators: Timer / AhCounter / Grammarian; Hosts: Guest Intro Host / TOM / TTM; Evaluators: TTE / IEs) plus overall agenda flow + atmosphere.
20. **Voting Section** — audience votes for awards (typically run by TOM or a dedicated voting host).
21. **Moment of Truth (MoT)** — generic TM = meeting feedback for officers; **at SoarHigh repurposed as 真情分享 (heart-to-heart sharing)** where anyone present is invited on stage to share feelings or reflections on any topic (3–5 min, optional).
22. **Awards** — winners announced (President typically presents).
23. **President's closing remarks** — 1–2 min. **The President's closing IS the meeting close** — the SAA does not deliver a separate closing at SoarHigh (their role is at the open, not the close).

**Key ordering note: Table Topics comes BEFORE Prepared Speeches at SoarHigh** — TT first warms up the room, then PS delivers the more rehearsed content. Some clubs run PS first; SoarHigh runs TT first.

The TOM is responsible for hand-offs between segments and for keeping the meeting on schedule.

## The bell signals (timing lights)

Every speaking role has a target **maximum** time. The Timer signals based on **how much time is LEFT**, not how much has been used:

- **Green card** — minimum threshold reached, speaker is now eligible for awards.
- **Yellow card** — target time approaching, wrap up.
- **Red card** — time's up. Speaker has a **30-second grace period** to conclude / close the speech.
- **Bell (rung at red + 30s)** — grace expired. Speaker must stop and give back the stage.

### The canonical card-timing rule

Let **T** = the target maximum length of the speech / segment. There are three bands:

| Band | Green card | Yellow card | Red card | Ring Bell |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **T ≤ 3 min** — Table Topics, most evaluations | T − 1 min | T − 30 sec | T | T + 30 sec |
| **3 min < T ≤ 10 min** — most prepared speeches, GE, TTE | T − 2 min | T − 1 min | T | T + 30 sec |
| **T > 10 min** — long speeches, workshops | T − 5 min | T − 2 min | T | T + 30 sec |

**Enforcement differs by setting:**
- **Formal Toastmasters contests** — the Timer does NOT ring the bell (silent timing). But any speech running past the red+30s threshold is **disqualified** from the contest. Strict.
- **Regular club meetings (including SoarHigh)** — the bell IS rung at red+30s, but it's a **soft requirement**: over-time speakers still participate in the meeting's awards voting. The bell mainly enforces time discipline / stage exit, not voting eligibility.

### Per-segment derived bands (rule applied to each segment's max)

| Role / segment | Green | Yellow | Red | Ring |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Table Topic speech | 1:00 | 1:30 | 2:00 | 2:30 |
| Individual Evaluation (IE) | 2:00 | 2:30 | 3:00 | 3:30 |
| Grammarian's Report | 2:00 | 2:30 | 3:00 | 3:30 |
| AhCounter's Report | 1:00 | 1:30 | 2:00 | 2:30 |
| Hark Master Pop Quiz | 3:00 | 4:00 | 5:00 | 5:30 |
| Prepared Speech (Pathways early) | 5:00 | 6:00 | 7:00 | 7:30 |
| Table Topics Evaluation (TTE), whole block | 6:00 | 7:00 | 8:00 | 8:30 |
| General Evaluation (GE), whole block | 6:00 | 7:00 | 8:00 | 8:30 |
| Prepared Speech (Pathways advanced) | T − 5 | T − 2 | T | T + 30s |
| Workshop speech | T − 5 | T − 2 | T | T + 30s |

The Timer reports actual durations near the end of the meeting. If a meeting uses a non-standard length, apply the canonical rule scaled to that length and have the Timer announce the cards explicitly in their opening.

## Evaluation segments (brief)

Three evaluation segments occur in a Regular meeting: TTE (after Table Topics), one IE per Prepared Speaker, and the GE (near the end). Each segment's bell timing is in the table above. **For the full evaluation methodology — sandwich structure, address-the-audience-first convention, Pathways anchoring, per-role prep, common pitfalls, contest rules — load `toastmasters-evaluation`.** This skill stays focused on meeting-flow and timing.

## Voting

Voting happens in a dedicated segment near the end of the meeting. **SoarHigh uses digital voting** via the club web app — audience (members + guests) cast votes from their phones and counts update live. Some other clubs still use paper ballots collected by the SAA.

Standard categories (typical, not exhaustive):

- Best Prepared Speaker
- Best Table Topic Speaker
- Best Evaluator
- Best Host
- Best Facilitator
- Best Supporter
- Best Meeting Manager
- Custom categories per meeting (workshop awards, themed awards, etc.)

Ties are common; clubs handle them differently (split the award, run-off vote, alphabetical tiebreak — varies).

## Robert's Rules of Order in Toastmasters meetings

In a regular speaking meeting (the focus of this skill), **Robert's Rules barely surface**. They mainly apply at the **officer election meeting** held twice a year — that's where motions, seconds, debate, votes, quorum, and majority thresholds actually drive the agenda. **For election-meeting flow and the working subset of Robert's Rules as applied at SoarHigh, load `soarhigh-officer-election`.**

If a member or guest wants to address a regular meeting outside an assigned role ("point of order", "point of information"), they should raise their hand and wait to be recognized by the TOM or chair — that minimal application is the only Robert's Rules touch in a regular meeting.

## Meeting types — Regular vs Workshop vs Custom

### Regular meeting
The flow above. Default Pathways skill development. ~90–100 minutes.

### Workshop meeting
Centers on a Workshop Speaker delivering an extended training session (30–60 min). Typically:
- Full opening (SAA, President, TOM intro)
- Workshop speaker (long block)
- Q&A or interactive segment
- Brief evaluation / feedback (no IE; sometimes a custom evaluator)
- Awards based on workshop participation rather than speech quality
- Standard close

Workshop meetings drop Prepared Speeches and IE. They may keep or drop Table Topics depending on time.

### Custom meeting
A club-specific format that doesn't fit Regular or Workshop. Examples: a debate night, a humor showcase, an evaluation contest, a guest-only meet & greet. The agenda is custom-built for the theme. Often skips one or more standard segments.

## How to handle delays / overruns

If the meeting is running long (a Prepared Speech ran 2 minutes over, a guest intro stretched), the TOM should:

1. Trim the next available flexible segment (often the break, or the GE's allotted time).
2. NOT cut a Prepared Speaker who hasn't gone yet.
3. NOT skip the evaluators — they are the educational core.
4. Possibly skip optional segments (Hark, MoT) at the end if absolutely necessary.

**Time discipline is everyone's responsibility** — every speaker, host, evaluator, and facilitator is expected to land within their bell band. The TOM coordinates pacing across segments, the Timer provides the signals, the GE audits the result, but staying on time is on each individual on stage. A meeting that runs long is a collective miss, not just the TOM's.
