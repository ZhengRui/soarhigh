# SoarHigh Club FAQ

A practical Q&A reference for SoarHigh members and guests. For formal policy or bylaws-level questions, load `soarhigh-bylaws` instead. For Toastmasters knowledge that isn't club-specific, load `toastmasters-roles` or `meeting-protocol`.

Anything not explicitly answered here should be deferred to the **VP Education**, **VP Membership**, or club officers — do not guess.

## Attending a meeting

When answering an explicit first-visit / how-to-attend question, include the [SoarHigh 邀请函](https://soarhigh.oss-cn-shenzhen.aliyuncs.com/public/miniapp/images/soarhigh_invitation_letter_after_March_2026.png). Do not repeat this link on follow-up questions about roles, voting, guest participation, what to prepare, what to bring, or 注意事项 if the link was already shared earlier in the visible conversation.

### Q: How do I attend my first SoarHigh meeting as a guest?
- SoarHigh meets **每周三晚上 7:30 – 9:30**. Default venue is **华美居装饰家居城 B 区 809（地铁 1 号线宝体站 B 口）**, with roughly every 1–2 months rotating to **前海党群服务中心 · 前海书院（地铁 5 号线桂湾站 / 1 号线鲤鱼门站）**. Confirm this week's venue in the **SoarHigh WeChat group** or the **〖搜嗨头马〗 miniapp** before heading out.
- Show up; introduce yourself if invited during the guest self-introduction segment (1 minute is fine).
- You don't need to do anything else — listen, ask questions during break, decide if you want to come back.
- Guests pay nothing to attend.
- For an at-a-glance club intro (会议地点、流程、时间、加入方式总览图), grab the [SoarHigh 邀请函](https://soarhigh.oss-cn-shenzhen.aliyuncs.com/public/miniapp/images/soarhigh_invitation_letter_after_March_2026.png). The 〖搜嗨头马〗 miniapp also surfaces this same image as "Get Soarhigh Playbook" in the check-in success modal — long-press to save it.

### Q: What should I bring?
- Yourself. Optionally a notebook if you want to capture ideas. Phones should be silenced once the meeting starts.
- Business cards if you'd like to network during break.

### Q: Will I be put on the spot?
- Not for a Prepared Speech. Possibly for Table Topics if you raise your hand, but the TTM almost always asks first. You can decline if you're not ready.

## Becoming a member

### Q: How do I become a member?
- Attend at least one meeting as a guest first to confirm you like the format.
- Speak with the **VP Membership** — they'll walk you through the application + dues.
- Once enrolled, you'll get a Toastmasters International login (Pathways access) and start being offered roles in upcoming meetings.

### Q: What does membership cost?
- **6 months: ¥750**
- **12 months: ¥1350**
- **One-time registration fee for new members: ¥200** (paid directly to Toastmasters International)
- Membership fees cover venue costs, refreshments, and other club activity costs. Full breakdown lives in `soarhigh-bylaws`.

### Q: What do I get out of membership?
- **Pathways access** — Toastmasters International's online education curriculum. Pick a path, work through projects across 5 levels, deliver speeches, get goal-aligned evaluations. (See `toastmasters-pathways`.)
- **A safe stage to practice** public speaking and leadership — a low-pressure room where imperfect ideas are welcome and growth is the point.
- **A dedicated mentor** assigned via the VPE — someone to guide your Pathways progression and give one-on-one feedback.
- **The SoarHigh family (温暖大家庭)** — beyond skill-building, SoarHigh is built around a family atmosphere. Members support, encourage, and celebrate each other; outings, themed special meetings (speed dating night, Halloween party, hiking, etc.), and 真情分享 segments make it feel like a community, not just a class.
- **The Toastmasters network** — over 80 clubs in Shenzhen alone, clubs in 140+ countries worldwide. Joining SoarHigh plugs you into a global network of speakers, leaders, and professionals; many members find friendships, business connections, and even partners through TM events.
- **Eligibility for in-meeting awards** (Best PS, Best Evaluator, etc.) and recognition of progress as you complete Pathways levels.

## Roles and signing up

### Q: How do I sign up for a role at the next meeting?
- The Meeting Manager for each meeting is responsible for filling roles. Reach out to that meeting's manager (visible on the agenda page).
- Or message the **VP Education** if you want to sign up for a Prepared Speech project — they coordinate the speech queue.

### Q: What roles should I try as a new member?
A reasonable progression:
1. Timer or AhCounter (low-pressure, builds familiarity with the format).
2. Grammarian (more responsibility, still small).
3. Table Topics speaker (no preparation needed, builds spontaneity).
4. Individual Evaluator (now you've watched many speeches).
5. Prepared Speaker (your first Pathways project — Ice Breaker).
6. Toastmaster of the Meeting (TOM) — once you're comfortable on stage.
7. General Evaluator — once you have several IE evaluations under your belt.

The VPE can recommend a tailored path.

### Q: I signed up but I can't make the meeting. What do I do?
- Tell the Meeting Manager AS SOON AS POSSIBLE so they can find a replacement. Don't wait until meeting day.

## During a meeting — practical questions

### Q: What if I don't know what to say in Table Topics?
- Take a breath. Repeat the prompt. Pick one specific angle (a person you know, a single moment, a short opinion). Don't try to give a complete answer — give an interesting small one.
- 1–2 minutes is shorter than you think.

### Q: Can guests vote?
- **For regular-meeting awards:** yes. Guests / non-members who are present may vote for in-meeting awards such as Best Prepared Speaker, Best Table Topics Speaker, Best Evaluator, etc. Voting is digital via the club app and includes members + guests.
- **For officer elections or club governance votes:** no. Those votes are for paid members only and may require quorum. If the user asks about officer elections, load `soarhigh-officer-election`; if the user asks about bylaws / finance / membership-policy decisions, load `soarhigh-bylaws`.
- If the user simply asks "Can guests vote?" / "嘉宾可以投票吗?", answer with this distinction. Do **not** answer "guests cannot vote" without qualifying the context.

### Q: How are awards decided?
- **At a SoarHigh regular meeting**: pure audience vote during the voting segment near the end. **Whoever gets the most votes wins** — no time-based disqualification, even if a speaker ran over the red card. Voting is digital via the club app (members + guests).
- **At formal Toastmasters contests** (different setting, not regular meetings): going past the red + 30s threshold **disqualifies** the speaker from the contest. Strict enforcement. The Timer signals silently in contests (no bell).

## Tools and platforms

### Q: What does this AI assistant do?
- The assistant is a multi-agent system. Specialists currently available:
  - **会议管理助手 (Meeting agent)** — edits / creates / saves meeting agenda drafts. Used by the meeting manager to plan an upcoming meeting. **Members only.**
  - **统计分析助手 (Statistics agent)** — answers historical questions: who has done which roles, attendance trends, awards counts, who organized which meeting. **Members only.**
  - **知识问答助手 (General Q&A agent, this one)** — answers questions about Toastmasters in general or SoarHigh club specifics from documentation. **Open to everyone, including non-members and guests.**
- Specialists planned / on the roadmap (members only):
  - **PPT 助手** — generate / edit meeting slide decks.
  - **海报助手** — generate meeting posters and event flyers.
  - **备稿演讲助手** — help members prepare and refine Pathways prepared speeches.
- **Access summary:** non-members and guests can use the 知识问答助手 only. All other specialists (current and upcoming) require membership and a bound club-member account.

### Q: Where do I find past meetings?
- The meetings page on the SoarHigh website (soarhigh.top) lists published meetings.
- The 〖搜嗨头马〗 miniapp also shows past meetings — same content, optimized for mobile.

## Pathways and education

### Q: How do I start Pathways?
- After enrollment, log in to Toastmasters International's website (login provided by the VPE / VPM after dues are processed). Take the path-recommendation quiz, pick a path, and start with the **Ice Breaker** project.

### Q: How do I schedule my first speech?
- Tell the **VP Education**. They schedule speeches across upcoming meetings based on the Pathways queue and meeting capacity.

### Q: Who picks my evaluator?
- The Meeting Manager assigns the IE based on availability and experience. Members can request a specific evaluator if there's someone they want feedback from — coordinate via the Meeting Manager.

## Communication

### Q: How do I reach the executive committee?
- **Directly on WeChat.** Officers are all active in the SoarHigh WeChat group — message any of them there, or add them as a contact and DM directly. See `soarhigh-bylaws` for the current officer roster (President / VPE / VPM / VPPR / Secretary / Treasurer / SAA).

### Q: Where do members coordinate between meetings?
- SoarHigh uses a member group chat for between-meeting coordination. Ask the **VP Membership** or any active member to be added.
