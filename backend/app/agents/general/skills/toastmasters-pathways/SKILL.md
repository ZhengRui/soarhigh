# Toastmasters Pathways

Pathways is Toastmasters International's modern education curriculum, introduced in 2018 to replace the legacy CC (Competent Communicator) and AC (Advanced Communicator) tracks. Members pick a "path" focused on a skill area, then complete projects within five progressive levels.

## Source and reliability note

The path / project matrix in this skill was compiled from the official Pathways one-pager **"Pathways Path and Project Matrix / 我的学习路径"**, dated **2025-03-27**. The PDF itself states that project details should follow the latest **Base Camp** materials, so **if Base Camp and this skill disagree, Base Camp wins**. Direct members to the Toastmasters International Pathways portal (https://www.toastmasters.org/education/pathways) and to their Base Camp account for canonical, up-to-the-minute project briefs.

## Legend

- `必修` = required project for that path.
- `选修` = elective project available for that path in the matrix.
- `—` = blank/not shown as available for that path in the source matrix.
- `⭐` = the project requires organizing or leading a team. For Level 4 and Level 5 team projects, the source notes that a team should normally have at least 4 people including the leader, unless the project has special requirements.
- `2+E` = two speeches plus one evaluator role.

## The 6 specialized paths (current)

Toastmasters International **simplified Pathways from 11 down to 6 specialized paths** (Effective Coaching, Innovative Planning, Strategic Relationships, Leadership Development, and Team Collaboration have been retired or merged). The 6 active paths:

| Code | 中文路径 | English Path | Focus / 培养方向 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **DL** | 动态领导 | Dynamic Leadership | 培养战略领导力和化解冲突的技能 |
| **VC** | 愿景沟通 | Visionary Communication | 培养创新沟通与领导技能 |
| **MS** | 激励策略 | Motivational Strategies | 培养激励型领导与沟通技能 |
| **PI** | 有说服力的影响 | Persuasive Influence | 培养在复杂情况下进行领导的技能 |
| **PM** | 精通演讲 | Presentation Mastery | 培养公众演讲技能 |
| **EH** | 运用幽默 | Engaging Humor | 培养公众演讲和演讲稿撰写技能 |

There is no "best" path — each builds general communication and leadership skills with a different specialization lens. New members typically take the path-recommendation quiz on the Toastmasters International website after enrolling, and the quiz suggests one of these six. Members can switch paths or take additional paths after completing one.

## Completion rules from the matrix

- A path requires at least **15 projects** to finish: **11 required + 4 electives**.
- Level 1: **4 required projects**.
- Level 2: **3 required projects**.
- Level 3: **1 required project + 2 electives**.
- Before unlocking Level 4, the member must complete these meeting roles: **Table Topics Master / 即兴演讲主持人 1次**, **Toastmaster of the Day / 总主持 1次**, and **Evaluator / 点评人 1次**.
- Level 4: **1 required project + 1 elective**.
- Level 5: **2 required projects + 1 elective**. `8510 反思你的学习路径` is the final required project.
- Leadership roles can be prepared up to about **3 months before completing Level 4**, according to the note in the matrix.

Each project is a **prepared speech with stated learning objectives**. The Individual Evaluator (IE) for that speech evaluates against THE PROJECT'S STATED OBJECTIVES, not generic speech criteria — this is the most important Pathways-vs-old-system distinction.

## 2026 vintage paths (newly added)

> **Provenance note:** This section is **not** from the 2025-03 Path / Project Matrix PDF cited at the top of this skill. It summarizes 2026 Toastmasters International announcements about additional vintage paths. Details may evolve — verify on the Pathways portal (https://www.toastmasters.org/education/pathways) or Base Camp before quoting authoritatively to a member.

In 2026, Toastmasters International added **two vintage paths** that revive foundational material from the legacy tracks:

- One vintage path includes the **Basic Training Manual** with original content from Toastmasters founder **Dr. Ralph C. Smedley**.
- The other vintage path consists of the **Competent Communication manual** plus two **Advanced Communication manuals**.

These vintage paths are **additive** — for members who want the original CC/AC-style content within the modern Pathways framework. The 6 specialized paths above remain the primary recommendation for new members.

## Legacy CC / AC (still seen in older agendas)

Before Pathways:

- **CC (Competent Communicator)** — 10 prepared speeches in the basic manual, completed in order.
- **AC (Advanced Communicator) Bronze / Silver / Gold** — tiers above CC, each with its own manual sequence.
- **CL (Competent Leader)** — leadership track.
- **ALB / ALS / DTM** — advanced leadership track culminating in legacy DTM.

Members who started before 2018 may still have CC / AC awards in their record. Active progression for new members should be on Pathways.

## Recent enhancements (2025–2026 updates)

- **More opportunities for meeting roles + educational presentations.** Each level now has a section outlining required meeting roles and how those roles contribute to learning.
- **Levels 3, 4, and 5** introduce members to the **Successful Club Series**, **Better Speaker Series**, and **Leadership Excellence Series** — short structured presentations members can deliver to support their club.

## How Pathways shows up in agenda role-takers

When agendas list a Prepared Speaker, the role-taker line typically includes the path code + level + project number, e.g. `Joyce Feng (DL L1P2)` = Joyce, Dynamic Leadership path, Level 1 Project 2. The IE for that speech needs:

- The **path** (DL / VC / MS / PI / PM / EH) — to find the project objectives.
- The **level** (L1–L5).
- The **project number / ID** (e.g. 8101) within that level.

Without those three facts the IE can't evaluate against the project's actual goals and falls back to "how was the speech in general", losing the Pathways advantage. The Meeting Manager collects this info from each speaker before the meeting (see `soarhigh-meeting-manager` for the prepared-speech info template).

## Mentor system at SoarHigh (informal pairing)

Separate from the formal PW Mentor track above: new SoarHigh members are paired with a mentor by the **VP Education** when they enroll. This informal mentor:

- Helps them pick a path matching their goals.
- Reviews speech drafts before delivery.
- Often serves as their first IE (Individual Evaluator) for early projects.
- Tracks their progress through Pathways.

If you're new and don't have a mentor, ask the VPE. Members who want to formalize the role can pursue PW Mentor (8311 → 8410 → 8500) and earn certification.

## How to use this skill when advising a member

1. Ask for or infer the member's path code: `DL`, `VC`, `MS`, `PI`, `PM`, or `EH`.
2. Identify the current level and completed project IDs.
3. For required projects, use the path-specific summary first.
4. For electives, use the project matrix and only recommend projects marked `选修` for that path.
5. Before advising Level 4 projects, remind the member about the three meeting-role requirements.
6. For team projects marked `⭐`, remind the member to plan a team, timeline, and evidence/reflection early.
7. When creating a plan, prefer projects that match the member's real goals: speaking confidence, humor, leadership, conflict resolution, interviewing, visual aids, event/project management, online meeting facilitation, etc.

- The **VPE** if they don't have a mentor yet.
