# SoarHigh Officer Election Meeting

A regular SoarHigh meeting is governed lightly — TT, PS, evaluations, awards. **The officer election meeting is the one exception** where Robert's Rules of Order genuinely matters. This skill covers what makes the election meeting different from a normal Regular meeting.

For the regular meeting flow, see `meeting-protocol`. For the officer roles being elected and SoarHigh's six-month term cadence, see `toastmasters-roles` and `soarhigh-bylaws`.

## When elections happen at SoarHigh

SoarHigh runs **six-month officer terms** (not the generic Toastmasters one-year cycle):

- **Term A**: July 1 – December 31 → election held in late June.
- **Term B**: January 1 – June 30 → election held in late December.

Elections happen **twice a year**, around the term boundary. The exact meeting date is set by the executive committee a few weeks in advance and announced in the club channel. This skill does not record specific dates — direct the user to the current officer team or the meeting schedule.

## Roles up for election

The standard executive committee is elected (see `soarhigh-bylaws` for the current roster):

- President
- VP Education (VPE)
- VP Membership (VPM)
- VP Public Relations (VPPR)
- Secretary
- Treasurer
- Sergeant At Arms (SAA)

The **Immediate Past President (IPP)** is not elected — it's automatically held by the most recently outgoing President.

## Pre-meeting nomination process

Before election day:

1. **Call for candidates** — typically 2–4 weeks ahead. The current VPE (or a designated nominating committee) opens a call to all paid members for self-nomination or peer-nomination per role.
2. **Slate compilation** — nominations are collected and compiled into a slate (one or more candidates per role). The slate is shared with members ahead of the meeting so people know who is running.
3. **Candidate prep** — candidates prepare a brief speech (~1–2 min) on why they want the role and what they'd focus on.

Members can also nominate **from the floor** at the meeting itself — the slate is not closed until the chair formally closes nominations on election day (see flow below).

## Election-day meeting flow

For the practical agenda, **the easiest reference is the last two officer-election meetings on record** — they capture SoarHigh's actual segment ordering, time allocations, and ceremonial details (gavel passing, candidate speech length, etc.) far better than a generic template. The Meeting Manager organizing the election should pull up those past agendas (via the meetings page or the 统计分析助手) and adapt rather than design from scratch.

The shapes below describe the two structural options at the high level:

### Shape A — pure election meeting
The whole meeting is the election. No PS / TT / IE. The agenda runs:

1. **SAA opens** — meeting rules, declares meeting open.
2. **President's opening remarks** — frames the term ending, thanks the outgoing officers.
3. **Roll call / quorum check** — Secretary confirms enough paid members are present to vote (per bylaws; typically one-third of paid members or the bylaws-defined quorum). **No quorum, no binding vote** — Robert's Rules is strict on this.
4. **Reading of the slate** — VPE (or chair) reads the candidates per role.
5. **Call for floor nominations** — chair invites additional nominations from the floor for each role. Each floor nomination needs a **second** before it's added to the slate.
6. **Motion to close nominations** — once no further nominations come, a member moves "I move to close nominations." Another seconds. Chair calls a voice vote (or show-of-hands); majority closes nominations.
7. **Candidate speeches** — each candidate speaks ~1–2 min in turn (usually alphabetical or in the order nominated). For uncontested roles, the single candidate still gives a brief speech.
8. **Voting per role** — voted role by role, in officer-rank order (President first, then VPE, VPM, VPPR, Secretary, Treasurer, SAA). Mechanics below.
9. **Counting + announcement** — Secretary or designated tellers count; chair announces the winner per role as results come in.
10. **Installation / passing of the gavel** — outgoing President hands the gavel to the new President; sometimes a brief installation ceremony reads each new officer's responsibilities.
11. **Closing remarks** — outgoing and incoming Presidents both speak briefly.

### Shape B — election attached to a regular meeting
A regular meeting runs (PS / TT / evaluations / awards), and the election segment is **inserted near the end**, replacing or shortening the GE / awards / closing segments. The election segment itself follows steps 3–10 above. This shape is more common when the club doesn't want to lose a full meeting to elections.

The executive committee picks Shape A or B per term based on workload and whether other business needs the full meeting.

## Voting mechanics for elections

Election voting differs from the regular-meeting award voting in two ways:

- **Eligibility** — only **paid members** vote in officer elections (guests cannot vote). Award voting in a regular meeting is open to members + guests; officer elections are not.
- **Mechanism** — typically a **secret ballot** (digital with no public live count, or paper). The award-voting digital app is sometimes reused with a closed-tally mode; otherwise paper ballots are issued and collected by tellers. Show-of-hands is allowed for uncontested roles but is uncommon for contested ones.

A simple majority of votes cast wins. If no candidate gets a majority on the first ballot in a multi-candidate race, the lowest vote-getter is dropped and a second ballot is held among the rest. Ties on a final ballot are broken per the club's bylaws (often a chair tiebreaker or a coin toss — the exact rule should be confirmed with the Secretary).

## Robert's Rules of Order — actually applied

In a regular meeting Robert's Rules barely surface. In the election meeting they're the operating manual:

- **Motions** — formal proposals start with "I move that …". Common motions on election day: *to nominate*, *to close nominations*, *to amend the slate*, *to adopt the slate by acclamation* (uncontested), *to adjourn*.
- **Seconds** — almost all motions need a second before they can be debated or voted on. No second → motion dies on the floor.
- **Debate** — the chair recognizes speakers in turn. For/against alternation is the convention. Debate has limits (typically 2 turns per member per motion, ~2 min each) — chair enforces.
- **Amendments** — motions can be amended; the amendment is voted on first ("amend → vote on amendment → vote on amended motion"). On election day, amendments mostly apply to the slate ("I move to amend the nomination of X by adding Y as well") — they're rare in practice.
- **Votes** — voice vote (aye/nay) for procedural motions; secret ballot for contested elections; show-of-hands for uncontested or simple yes/no.
- **Quorum** — must be confirmed before any binding vote. SoarHigh's specific quorum count is in the bylaws (held by the Secretary); see `soarhigh-bylaws`.
- **Majority** — simple majority (>50% of votes cast) for routine motions and election outcomes. **Two-thirds majority** for bylaw amendments, expulsion of a member, or anything reopening a previously decided question.
- **Point of order / Point of information** — any member can interrupt with these to flag a procedural issue or ask a question; chair rules immediately. Used sparingly.

The chair (the outgoing President or a designated election chair) is the one applying these rules in real time. They don't need to quote rule numbers — they just need to keep the flow of "motion → second → debate → vote" intact.

## What changes the meeting after election day

The first regular meeting after elections has a few transitions:

- **New officer roster** announced in opening remarks.
- **New SAA** opens the meeting (instead of the outgoing one).
- **New President** delivers opening remarks (the old President becomes IPP).
- **Officer roster updates** in club assets — the website officer page, default agenda templates, AI-assistant references, and any printed materials all need to reflect the new term. This is a Secretary / VPPR follow-up handled in the days after elections, not an election-day item.

Officer training (typically a separate session organized by the District) usually happens within a few weeks of the new term starting; it's not part of the election meeting itself.
